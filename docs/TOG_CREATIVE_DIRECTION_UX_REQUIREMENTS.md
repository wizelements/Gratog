# Taste of Gratitude — Creative Direction + UI/UX + Marketing Psychology Requirements

> **Status**: MANDATORY — Governs every implementation decision
> **Created**: June 3, 2026
> **Scope**: Layout, copy, hierarchy, interactions, navigation, checkout, menus, admin UX, mobile, visual design, content structure, photography, CTA strategy, product organization, onboarding, emotional pacing

---

## Design Gate — Every Section Must Answer

- Why is this here?
- What emotional purpose does it serve?
- What business purpose does it serve?
- Does it reduce or increase cognitive load?
- Does it increase trust?
- Does it reinforce the founder story?
- Does it reinforce weekly ritual behavior?
- Does it support the market-first business model?

## 2. Visual Hierarchy Requirements

### Current Platform Failures (from audit):

- Competing CTAs
- Badge overload (sparkle icons, gradient text, pill badges)
- Visual noise
- Too many sections fighting for attention
- Weak hierarchy
- "Everything important" syndrome

### Every page MUST have:

- **ONE** primary action
- **ONE** emotional focus
- Clear reading flow
- Breathing room
- Visual pacing

### Users should NEVER feel:

- Overwhelmed
- Rushed
- Pressured
- Lost
- Distracted

### Use intentionally:

- Spacing
- Typography
- Contrast
- Imagery
- Animation (sparingly)
- Gradients (sparingly)
- Badges (sparingly)

### Remove aggressively:

- Unnecessary pills/badges
- Cluttered overlays
- Excessive drop shadows
- Animation spam
- CTA duplication
- Visual competition

## 3. Marketing Psychology Principles

### Build trust through:

- Honesty
- Warmth
- Consistency
- Founder visibility
- Operational clarity
- Sensory realism
- Community feeling
- Routine reinforcement

### NOT through:

- Fake urgency
- Fake scarcity
- Fake social proof
- Manipulative countdowns
- Exaggerated health promises
- Pressure-heavy copy

### A. Trust Transfer

The market relationship transfers into digital trust.

Customers should feel:
> "I've met these people." / "I trust this brand." / "This feels real."

### B. Ritual Psychology

Frame products as part of weekly routines.

✅ Good: "your weekly pickup", "part of your rhythm", "freshly prepared for the week", "crafted in small batches"

❌ Bad: "optimize your body", "supercharge your life", "detox now"

### C. Cognitive Ease

Reduce decision fatigue with:
- Guided flows
- Clear categories
- Progressive disclosure
- Intentional defaults
- Concise product descriptions

### D. Calm Conversion

Users should feel invited, not pushed. Best conversion strategy:
- Reassurance
- Authenticity
- Clarity
- Emotional resonance

### E. Community Reinforcement

Markets and repeat customers should feel visible.
> "People actually come here every week."

## 5. Homepage UX — Emotional Flow

### Recommended section order (psychology-driven):

1. **Founder-led Hero** — trust first
2. **Weekly Menu** — "what's fresh this week"
3. **Story** — context before selling
4. **Market Schedule** — local trust anchor
5. **Product Categories** — guided discovery
6. **Educational Section** — ingredient transparency
7. **Newsletter / Weekly Rhythm Reminder** — habit loop

### Why this order:

- Trust before products
- Context before checkout
- Story before selling
- Menus before catalog overload

### Homepage must NOT feel:

- Endless
- Chaotic
- Stuffed with features
- Over-engineered

## 7. Mobile UX Requirements

### This platform is fundamentally mobile-first.

Primary traffic sources: QR scans, Instagram, market interactions, phones.

### Prioritize:

- Thumb reach
- Readability
- Fast scanning
- Image clarity
- Menu clarity
- Simple navigation
- Low friction

### Reduce:

- Floating elements
- Stacked sticky bars
- Popup overload
- Competing notifications
- Unnecessary animations

---

## 8. Menu UX Requirements

### Menus are central to brand identity.

Weekly menus should visually feel like:
> "fresh drops from the market"

### Requirements:

- Beautiful image presentation
- Easy zooming
- Fast loading
- Mobile readability
- Direct order paths
- Clear market association

### Do NOT:

- Bury menus
- Make menus feel secondary
- Treat menus like PDFs hidden in a footer

---

## 9. Market Experience UX

### Markets are the emotional center of the brand.

The site should encourage:
> "come meet us"

NOT:
> "replace the physical experience entirely"

### Support:

- Market photography
- Booth imagery
- Weekly setup visuals
- "This weekend at Serenbe" moments
- Live/active indicators if feasible

---

## 10. Checkout UX Requirements

### Checkout should feel: calm, trustworthy, simple, reassuring.

### Users must ALWAYS understand:

- What they ordered
- Where they're picking up
- When they're picking up
- Whether they've paid
- What happens next

### Final confirmation emotional target:

> "Your order is reserved and we're preparing for you."

### Avoid:

- Confusing totals
- Unclear delivery logic
- Hidden fees
- Overloaded forms
- Unclear preorder behavior

---

## 11. Admin UX Requirements

### Admin should feel: operational, clear, practical, fast, dependable.

### Admin users should quickly understand:

- Active menus
- Active products
- Current markets
- Today's preorders
- Fulfillment status
- Visibility state
- What customers currently see publicly

### NOT:

- Developer-centric
- Overcomplicated
- Cluttered

---

## 12. Accessibility + Inclusivity

### Required:

- Sufficient contrast
- Readable typography
- Alt text on all images
- Keyboard navigation
- Focus states
- Semantic HTML structure
- Reduced motion support (`prefers-reduced-motion`)
- Screen-reader friendliness

> Do not sacrifice accessibility for aesthetics.

---

## 13. Final Creative Standard

### The platform should feel like:

> "A real person rebuilt their life, turned that journey into nourishment for others, and created a weekly community ritual around gratitude and consistency."

### Emotional pillars:

- Humanity
- Rhythm
- Care
- Freshness
- Rebuilding
- Calm confidence
- Intentional living
- Community trust

### Implementation test:

Every page, component, CTA, workflow, image, interaction, and admin system should support that emotional direction.

- Nothing should feel accidental.
- Nothing should feel generic.
- Nothing should feel emotionally disconnected from the founder story.
